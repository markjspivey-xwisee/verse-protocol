# The Cinder Library

**Type:** Location
**Parent:** Ashenmere
**Relation:** extends

---

The Cinder Library is the largest repository of knowledge in Ashenmere — a vast, cavernous structure where books are burned to be read, and the ash patterns on the walls constitute the catalog.

## Architecture

The Library is built into a dormant volcanic caldera, its chambers carved from obsidian and lined with ceramic tiles that capture and preserve ash-text. The structure descends seven levels deep, each level corresponding to an era of Ashenmere's history:

1. **The Living Stacks** — the surface level, where new texts are burned daily and fresh ash accumulates on purpose-built reading walls
2. **The Smoke Halls** — where the rising smoke from lower levels is channeled through narrow flues, allowing scholars to "overhear" burns happening elsewhere in the Library
3. **The Gray Archive** — the primary research level, walls dense with centuries of layered ash-text, readable with careful technique
4. **The Compression** — where ash has been pressed by weight and time into thin, dense sheets that hold extraordinary information density
5. **The Whisper Strata** — ash so old it has partially mineralized, requiring specialized tools to read
6. **The Silence** — a level where ash has fully petrified into stone, its meaning locked inside mineral structure. Only the Archivist has read from this level.
7. **The Foundation** — sealed. No one living has been there. The Archivist believes it contains the first words ever spoken in Ashenmere.

## Operations

The Library employs three orders:

- **Burners** — who select, prepare, and ignite texts with precision, controlling flame color and intensity to produce the clearest possible ash-speech
- **Readers** — who interpret ash patterns on the walls, catalog new deposits, and maintain the cross-reference system
- **Preservers** — who seal and protect ash-text from wind, moisture, and the careless touch of visitors

## The Paradox

The Library's great irony: to preserve a book, you must destroy it. Every text that enters the Cinder Library is burned. The original physical form is sacrificed so its content can be spoken by fire and recorded in ash. Authors who donate texts to the Library understand they are giving their work a different kind of permanence — not the durability of paper, but the persistence of settled ash.

Some scholars argue this makes the Cinder Library the most honest institution in Ashenmere: it admits that all preservation is transformation.
